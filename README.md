# E-commerce Strategy Analysis: Growth vs Profit

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes whether an e-commerce startup should prioritize rapid growth or focus on profitability. Using transactional and sales data, the analysis evaluates revenue trends, customer base, and profit margins to determine the most sustainable business strategy.

## 🎯 Business Problem
This analysis evaluates whether an early-stage e-commerce startup should prioritize aggressive growth or focus on short-term profitability. The objective is to assess revenue stability, customer behavior, and unit economics to determine the most strategically sound direction.

## 🛠 Tools & Skills
- Microsoft Excel  
- Data Cleaning  
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)  
- Financial Analysis  
- Data Visualization  

## 📊 Analysis Conducted
- Revenue analysis  
- Profit margin analysis  
- Customer acquisition cost evaluation  
- Product-level performance analysis  
- Trend analysis over time  

## 🔍 Key Insights
- From an economic perspective, the decision between growth and profitability involves opportunity cost and risk-return trade-offs.  
- Customer behavior metrics revealed strong retention dynamics  
- Despite strong retention, the startup is not expanding its customer base. Revenue volatility appears linked to insufficient acquisition momentum rather than customer churn.  
- High repeat purchase rates indicate strong product–market fit. Customers are returning and generating sustained value, suggesting the core offering resonates with the market.
  

## ✅ Recommendations
-The startup should prioritize disciplined growth over short-term profitability optimization.
Rationale:
-Strong retention and repeat purchase behavior confirm product–market fit.
-Weak acquisition is limiting scale and contributing to revenue instability.
-Economic analysis supports growth investment provided CLV remains meaningfully higher than CAC.
Focusing solely on profitability at this stage may stabilize margins temporarily but risks long-term stagnation and reduced market positioning.
Disciplined growth, supported by careful CAC monitoring, represents the optimal strategic direction.
